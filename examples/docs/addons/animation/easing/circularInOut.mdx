# circularInOut: Symmetric Circular Arc Motion Function

### Mathematical Foundation for Symmetric Circular Motion

The `circularInOut` function applies circular arc transformation through piecewise construction, creating symmetric motion profiles with circular acceleration and deceleration characteristics derived from quarter-circle geometry.

$$
f(t) = \begin{cases}
\frac{1}{2}(1 - \sqrt{1 - 4t^2}) & \text{if } t < 0.5 \\
\frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{(3-2t)(2t-1)} + 1) & \text{if } t \geq 0.5
\end{cases}
$$

The piecewise definition maintains continuity at $t = 0.5$ while generating circular motion profiles through quarter-circle arc scaling that creates distinctive curved visual characteristics.

### Circular Arc Symmetry Properties

The derivative reveals symmetric circular acceleration and deceleration patterns based on quarter-circle arc geometry with smooth transitions.

$$
\frac{df}{dt} = \begin{cases}
\frac{2t}{\sqrt{1 - 4t^2}} & \text{if } t < 0.5 \\
\frac{1 - t}{\sqrt{(3-2t)(2t-1)}} & \text{if } t \geq 0.5
\end{cases}
$$

This creates symmetric velocity curves that follow circular arc geometry, producing distinctive motion dynamics unavailable through polynomial or exponential transformations.
